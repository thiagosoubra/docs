Acknowledgments

It has now been five years since this book was first published, in English. During this time I left the Conscientiocentric Organization (CO) with whom I volunteered in Europe for 13 years to join the Brazil-based Interassistantail Services for the Internationalization of Conscientiology (ISIC). Sincere thanks to Jeffrey Lloyd for being a constant source of support during this period of change. I look forward to working with he and the ISIC team to create greater awareness of the Brazil-based COs globally.

Reflecting on the experience of writing and promoting this book, there were many kind people—colleagues, family, friends and even strangers—whose knowledge, time, effort, energy and sacrifices inspired me to start the project and bring it to this point where Less Incomplete is now available in several foreign languages—raising awareness of Conscientiology in countries such as Turkey, Romania and France. A special thank you to Dr. Waldo Vieira (deceased) for endorsing the book and to Patricia Sousa and Rodrigo Medeiros for their selfless assistance on so many levels and occasions.

Not all of the people who supported this project are physical. I would like to acknowledge the nonphysical Helpers who kept me focused, determined and confident of being able to overcome the challenges that inevitably present themselves with such a long-term endeavour. It has been one of the greatest privileges of my life to share this unpredictable but immensely rewarding experience with them and to develop such a deep level of mutual trust.

I owe a special debt of gratitude to my mother, Julie Gustus, and sister, Maryanne Gustus, for being there for me, unconditionally.
